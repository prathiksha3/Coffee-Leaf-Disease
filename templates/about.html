About Coffee Diseases

Rust

Prevention: Ensure proper spacing between coffee plants to improve air circulation, as rust thrives in humid conditions. Avoid excessive overhead irrigation, as wet foliage can promote rust development.

Treatment: Apply fungicides labeled for rust control as a preventive measure or at the first sign of infection. Follow manufacturer instructions carefully.

Black Rot

Prevention: Maintain good farm hygiene by removing and destroying infected leaves promptly. Ensure proper drainage to prevent waterlogging, as black rot thrives in waterlogged conditions.

Treatment: Apply copper-based fungicides early in the season as a preventive measure. Regularly inspect plants for signs of infection and apply fungicides if detected.

Brown Eye Spot

Prevention: Prune trees to improve air circulation and sunlight penetration within the canopy. Avoid overhead irrigation to minimize leaf wetness, which can contribute to disease development.

Treatment: Apply fungicides containing active ingredients like copper or mancozeb at regular intervals during the growing season. Remove and destroy infected leaves to reduce disease spread.
